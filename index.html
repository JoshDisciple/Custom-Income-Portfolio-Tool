2. Glossary & Definitions

Distribution Rate (Yield)
The annualised distribution (dividend) rate expressed as a percentage of the fundâ€™s value. A 3.9% yield means the fund pays distributions equal to 3.9% of its value each year.
Average Annual Growth Rate
The average yearly increase in value of an investment over a specified period, expressed as a percentage. It reflects the mean annual rate at which an investment has grown, not accounting for compounding, and is useful for comparing the historical performance of different assets.
Expense Ratio
The annual fee that funds charge to cover operating expenses, expressed as a percentage of assets. Lower expense ratios mean more of your money remains invested.
DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan)
A setting that automatically reinvests distributions back into the holding rather than paying them out. Enabling DRIP can accelerate growth through compounding.
Weighted Distribution Yield
The sum of each holdingâ€™s distribution yield weighted by its portfolio weight. It reflects the overall distribution rate of the portfolio.

Income Frequencies
